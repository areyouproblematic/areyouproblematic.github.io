Are You Problematic?

The easier it is to find sources to support your arguments, the more smoothly these conversations will go (relatively speaking) -- that's where we come in. New and better information is always welcome, and that's why our site will be constantly under construction. We'll make it a priority to update and add more content as our busy lives allow. DACA

Myth: These immigrants are violent criminals.

Fact: Any person accepted into DACA cannot have been convicted of a felony or significant misdemeanor. Source

Myth: They should become citizens the legal way like everyone else.

Fact: “Current law does not grant any legal status for the class of individuals who are current recipients of DACA. Recipients of DACA are currently unlawfully present in the U.S. with their removal deferred.” DACA allows recipients who pay into the program ($500 a year) to work in the US legally without the fear of deportation. It does not at this point offer any path to citizenship. To gain full citizenship, an undocumented DACA recipient would either have to leave the US for several years and begin the immigration process from the start or marry a current US citizen. Source Credit

Myth: DACA recipients don't pay taxes.

Fact: The 1.3 million young undocumented immigrants enrolled or immediately eligible for DACA contribute an estimated $2 billion a year in state and local taxes. This includes personal income, property, and sales and excise taxes. Source

Myth: DACA recipients are going to college on taxpayer’s money.

Fact: DACA students are not eligible for federal aid, although some states allow them to pay in-state tuition and colleges can provide scholarships. Source
